Essay: The Resurrection of Epistolary Storytelling in a Digital Era

Posted on March 25, 2025 | Crossover

Epistolary novels—stories told through collections of letters—were once a dominant literary form. From Samuel Richardson's "Pamela" to Bram Stoker's "Dracula," the format allowed for intimate first-person accounts from multiple perspectives. This form gradually fell from prominence as literary tastes evolved.

Today, we're witnessing a surprising revival of epistolary storytelling adapted for the digital age. Email exchanges, text messages, social media posts, and other digital communications have become the modern equivalent of letter collections...
